Context Map

A global view of the system. Who depends on whom and how.

Why do you need a map?

Class diagrams show code details. Context Map shows organizational and integration boundaries.

It answers questions:

  • What systems exist?
  • Who initiates changes (Upstream)?
  • Who is the consumer (Downstream)?
  • Where are the "political" team boundaries?

Upstream (U) and Downstream (D)

â¬†ï¸ Upstream (U): The flow comes FROM HERE. This team dictates the rules (e.g., publishes events). It influences others.

â¬‡ï¸ Downstream (D): The flow comes TO HERE. This team depends on U. If U breaks the API, D suffers.

On the map, this is drawn as an arrow from U to D, or with labels `U` and `D` at the ends of the connection.

How to draw?

Just circles and lines. The main thing is to label the type of relationship.
